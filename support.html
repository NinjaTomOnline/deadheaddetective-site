Quick checks before contacting support

Auto-tracking Auto-tracking depends on location and motion permissions being available to the app.
Alerts Trip-start, deadhead, and summary notifications require notification permission plus the related in-app toggles.
Data tools Export, retention, and delete-all-data controls live in Settings > Data & Privacy.

Fast troubleshooting

  • If trips are not starting automatically, confirm Auto-Tracking is on and the app still has location and motion access.
  • If trip-start or deadhead alerts are not appearing, confirm notifications are allowed in iOS and the alert toggles are still enabled in the app.
  • If route history looks incomplete, reopen the app after granting permissions so the tracking runtime can refresh and resume normal operation.
  • If History or Insights look empty on a fresh install, record at least one trip before expecting trend views to populate.
  • If export files do not appear, rerun Export Data from Settings and wait for the share sheet before leaving the app.

Permissions the app may need

  • Location: used to detect trips, map routes, and measure paid versus deadhead miles.
  • Motion & Activity: used to improve trip start and stop detection while reducing unnecessary tracking noise.
  • Notifications: used for trip-start alerts, deadhead warnings, and session summaries when enabled.

Support Request Checklist

What to include in a support report

Concrete reports make it much easier to tell whether the issue is permissions, trip detection, or route/session handling.

Device details

  • Your device model
  • Your iOS or iPadOS version
  • The app version you are using

What you expected

  • Whether the issue happened during an active trip, after a trip ended, or inside History or Insights
  • Which alert, route, or session behavior you expected to see

What happened instead

  • What the app actually showed
  • Whether the issue happens every time or only sometimes
